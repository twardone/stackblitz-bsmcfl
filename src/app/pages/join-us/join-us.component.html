The Buffalo Soldiers Motorcycle Club Tampa Bay Chapter of Florida has been established to foster unity and pride among brother and sister members. We are committed to projecting a positive image of ourselves by always showing respect for each other and the community at large. Together we are stronger than we are alone. Our Chapter can only thrive through unity.

Purpose

In all our club activities, members must always ensure their actions reflect our purpose:

  • Educate and keep alive the history of the Buffalo Soldiers, and other Minority Military contributions, from the Revolutionary War to present.
  • Provide positive role models to the youth within our community.
  • Assist charitable cause organizations through charitable giving and volunteering

Our goals are simple: to project a positive image of ourselves as bikers, to represent and uphold the honor of the Buffalo Soldiers, and to share the legacy and history with the community. We raise funds for those in need, encourage and support education, and promote positive and productive futures for youth. Please visit our website for the list of charities we support.

Guidelines for BSMC Membership

How to become a BSMC Member

All motorcycle riders interested in becoming a member of the Buffalo Soldiers Motorcycle Club of Florida Tampa Bay Chapter must meet with the club’s Vice President (VP) immediately. After meeting with the VP, the rider will decide if he/she wants to continue as a Prospect. The Prospect Stage is at least a three‐ month time frame in which three Regularly Scheduled Meetings must be attended. Should the Prospect decide to continue after those meetings, he/she will be voted in as a Probationary Member at the fourth meeting attended.

Prospective ("Prospect")

A prospective member (also called “prospect” or “hang around”) is an individual who is considering applying for membership into the organization. The hang around period for prospect is a minimum of three months. This gives the individual time to consider whether he/she can fulfill the commitment required by the NABSTMC and the Florida Tampa Bay Chapter. This period is also for socialization between the prospective member and the full patch members. During this time, the prospect is not obligated to the organization in any way nor is the organization obligated to the Prospect.

However, the prospect is encouraged to participate in club activities and rides as much as possible. The prospect must have a licensed and insured motorcycle with the proper endorsements on his/her driver’s license before participating. The prospect is required to purchase an approved black leather vest. The vest shall not bear any patches except for a name patch on the front.

Probation ("Probie") Member

The Probation stage is a six month time frame where the Probie proves dedication to the club through consistent participation in events and meetings (no exceptions). This stage has more logistical and time dedication to prove interest in the club than the Prospect stage. The VP will assign each Probie only one Sponsor who will answer questions and provide details regarding club rides/events and the required dress code to participate.

See below for a few of the high-level requirements:

  • Own a road ready, two or three wheeled distance motorcycle that is 750cc or higher, which is capable of maintaining highway speeds for sustained periods of time.
  • Be of good moral character.
  • Educate yourself and others on the history of the Buffalo Soldiers.
  • Actively support the club and its functions.
  • Wear the colors given to you by club to all meetings, motorcycle events, and anytime you represent the club – unless requested not to do so.
  • Complete 2,500 Buffalo Soldier miles and record it on the club issued mileage card.
  • Buffalo Soldier miles consist of miles ridden with the club or events sanctioned by the club, and all miles must be ridden on a motorcycle (2 or 3 wheeled).

    • 500 miles can be completed in a variety of ways including riding with one club member to and from social activities, biker events, parties, and/or meetings. Documentation of these rides must be verified after each ride.
    • 2,000 of the miles must be on rides that are established on the club-riding calendar. The official club-riding calendar is the calendar posted with the regular meeting minutes or sent out monthly by the Road Captain or President.
    • Two of the rides chosen from the club-calendar must be overnight events where the Probie stays overnight and participates in all event activities. If the event has a dance, the Probie must stay until the dance is over. In-state overnight must be at least 200 miles away from the Probie’s residence, unless attending a Club sanctioned event hosted by a Florida BSMC Chapter (not Tampa Bay). At least one overnight stay must be out of the state of Florida. The Probie can complete two out-of-state overnight trips to meet the two overnight rides requirement.
